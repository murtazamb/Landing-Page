Fixture for UR5e Automated Sub-Assembly

This project involved designing and fabricating a custom fixture for a UR5e collaborative robot used in an automated sub-assembly process. The fixture ensured accurate, repeatable part positioning and reduced setup variation; I served as team lead, coordinating design decisions and fabrication.

Overview

  • Goal: Create a rigid, repeatable fixture that positions parts consistently for a UR5e automated sub-assembly task.
  • Constraints: Maintain clear robot approach paths, prevent collisions, allow quick loading/unloading, and ensure stable part constraint allowing limited degrees of freedom.
  • My role: Team lead — led design reviews, delegated tasks, and worked hands-on in fabrication, assembly, and communicated with other teams about design specifications.
  • Approach: Defined Design requirments and constraints → Intial CAD design → Reworked and created a better Design → Created Prototype → Tested Aligment on the UR5e Robot → Submitted design for final manufacturing.

What I Learned

  • I learned about the basics of fixture and jig development. This involved economic factors, degrees of freedom, 3-2-1 fixture design principle.
  • How to design for manufacturability and robot access (tool clearance, approach angles, and fast part loading) while leading a team under time constraints.

Results

  • Improved workflow by supporting fast and easy loading while maintaining stable constraints and convenient robot and user access.
