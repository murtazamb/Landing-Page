Fixture for UR5e Automated Sub-Assembly

This project involved designing and fabricating a custom fixture for a UR5e collaborative robot used in an automated sub-assembly process. The fixture ensured accurate, repeatable part positioning and reduced setup variation; I served as team lead, coordinating design decisions and fabrication.

Overview

  • Goal: Create a rigid, repeatable fixture that positions parts consistently for a UR5e automated sub-assembly task.
  • Constraints: Maintain clear robot approach paths, prevent collisions, allow quick loading/unloading, and ensure stable part constraint without over-clamping.
  • Your role: Team lead — led design reviews, delegated tasks, and worked hands-on in fabrication, assembly, and final fit-up in the workcell.
  • Approach: Defined datum scheme → CAD layout of locating features → fabricated prototype → tested in the workcell → iterated to improve alignment and accessibility.

What I Learned

  • How datum selection and basic fixturing principles directly affect robotic repeatability and cycle stability.
  • How to design for manufacturability and robot access (tool clearance, approach angles, and fast part loading) while leading a team under time constraints.
