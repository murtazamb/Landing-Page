Category: CAD / Manufacturing / Structural Analysis

Ultralight Multi-Function Bicycle Wrench

Designed and manufactured an ultralight aluminum wrench required for biking that meets strict weight, torque, and size constraints while integrating a secondary tire-lever function. I focused on CAD design, by-hand stress analysis, FEA validation, and manufacturing-driven design refinement.

Overview

  • Goal: Design an ultralight wrench capable of applying 250 in-lb torque to an M8 fastener.
  • Constraints: Must be less than 32.6 g mass, the handle length must be less than 5 inches and must be machinable with manual milling.
  • Your role: CAD modeling, by-hand stress analysis, FEA interpretation, manufacturing input.
  • Approach: Concept generation → hand calculations → FEA → iterative redesign → machining.

What I Learned

  • Applying beam theory and stress analysis to validate lightweight structural designs.
  • Using FEA to identify stress concentrations and guide geometry refinement.
  • Designing for machining by adapting geometry to available end mills, tolerances, and manual milling constraints.

Results

  • Achieved a final wrench mass of ~27.7 g, comfortably below the 32.6 g requirement.
  • Withstood the required 250 in-lb torque on an M8 fastener without permanent deformation.
  • FEA showed a maximum von Mises stress of ~76 MPa, remaining below allowable stress limits.
  • Passed three-point bend testing with only elastic deformation and no structural failure.
