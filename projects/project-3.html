Category: CAD / Manufacturing / Structural Analysis

Ultralight Multi-Function Bicycle Wrench

Designed and manufactured an ultralight aluminum wrench for Extra-Ultralight Biking that meets strict weight, torque, and size constraints while integrating a secondary tire-lever function. I focused on CAD design, by-hand stress analysis, FEA validation, and manufacturing-driven design refinement.

Overview

  • Goal: Design an ultralight wrench capable of applying 250 in-lb torque to an M8 fastener.
  • Constraints: ≤32.6 g mass, ≥5 in handle length, machinable with manual milling.
  • Your role: CAD modeling, by-hand stress analysis, FEA interpretation, manufacturing input.
  • Approach: Concept generation → hand calculations → FEA → iterative redesign → machining.

What I Learned

  • Applying beam theory and stress analysis to validate lightweight structural designs.
  • Balancing FEA optimization with real manufacturing limitations and tooling constraints.

Results

  • Final mass: ~27.7 g, well below the 32.6 g requirement.
  • Max von Mises stress ~76 MPa, remaining below allowable stress limits.
