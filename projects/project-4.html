Project 4
Category: Mechanical Design / Vehicle Systems / Braking

Mass Mileage Brake System Integration

Designed a lightweight braking solution for the UMass Mass Mileage ultra-efficient vehicle ( to compete in Shell Eco-marathon), integrating bicycle disc brake hardware with the car’s wheel assembly. Worked in a team of four to develop the concept, model the mechanism, and ensure reliable braking with minimal added weight and size.

Overview

  • Goal: Create a reliable, lightweight brake system for an ultra-efficient vehicle competing in Shell Eco-marathon.
  • Constraints: Tight packaging around the wheel, low mass, low drag, simple manufacturable parts, and safe braking performance.
  • Team: Collaborated with 3 teammates (team of four total) to iterate on design choices and integration.
  • Your role: Contributed to concept development, CAD modeling, fit checks/clearances, and design iterations for mounting and linkage.
  • Approach: Requirements → concept sketches → CAD integration → iterative refinement for fit and function.

What I Learned

  • How to translate a rough concept sketch into a constrained CAD assembly with realistic mounting and clearances.
  • How to integrate off-the-shelf bicycle brake hardware into a custom vehicle architecture.
  • Improved design iteration habits by testing fit, adjusting geometry, and rechecking constraints quickly.
  • Strengthened teamwork skills by dividing tasks and combining feedback into a single integrated design.

Results

  • Produced an integrated brake concept that mounts bicycle disc brake hardware onto the Mass Mileage pedal assembly, using a pin mechanism to activate the brakes' hydraulics.
  • Developed a bracket + linkage layout that prioritizes low mass.
  • Created a CAD assembly suitable for further prototyping, manufacturing review, and iterative testing.
